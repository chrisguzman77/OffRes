# Sheltering Guide

## Sheltering in Place
Sheltering in place means staying inside your current location rather than evacuating.

### When to Shelter in Place
- During a tornado warning.
- During a chemical or radiation release.
- When authorities specifically advise sheltering in place.
- When roads are impassable or evacuation is more dangerous than staying.

### How to Shelter in Place
- Close and lock all windows and doors.
- Turn off fans, air conditioning, and heating systems that draw air from outside.
- If chemical or radiation event: seal windows, doors, and vents with plastic sheeting and duct tape. Move to an interior room with the fewest windows.
- Fill bathtubs and sinks with water in case water service is interrupted.
- Monitor battery-powered radio or TV for updates.
- Stay calm. Keep children occupied with games or activities.

## Evacuation Shelters
- Red Cross and FEMA operate emergency shelters during disasters.
- Shelters provide basic necessities: water, food, blankets, basic medical care.
- Bring with you: medications, important documents, phone chargers, personal hygiene items, change of clothes, comfort items for children.
- Shelters may not allow pets. Look for pet-friendly shelters or make arrangements in advance.
- Register at the shelter so family members can locate you.

## Setting Up a Safe Room
A safe room is a reinforced room designed to withstand extreme winds.
- Best locations: basement or ground-floor interior room.
- No windows.
- Reinforce walls with plywood or steel.
- Stock with emergency supplies: water, food, first aid kit, flashlight, battery radio, phone charger.
- Practice getting to the safe room quickly with all household members.
