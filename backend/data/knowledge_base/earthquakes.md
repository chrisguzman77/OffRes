# Earthquake Safety Guide

## Before an Earthquake
- Secure heavy furniture, water heaters, and appliances to walls.
- Store heavy objects on lower shelves.
- Know where to take cover in each room: under a sturdy desk or table, or against an interior wall away from windows.
- Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, first aid supplies, flashlight, and medications.
- Know how to shut off gas, water, and electricity.
- Identify safe spots and hazardous areas in your home.

## During an Earthquake
- DROP, COVER, and HOLD ON.
  - DROP to your hands and knees to prevent being knocked down.
  - COVER your head and neck under a sturdy desk or table. If no shelter is available, crouch near an interior wall.
  - HOLD ON to your shelter until the shaking stops.
- Stay away from windows, outside doors, and walls.
- Do not stand in a doorway — this is a myth. Doorways are no stronger than any other part of a modern house.
- If you are in bed, stay there and cover your head with a pillow.
- If you are outside, move to a clear area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
- If you are driving, pull over, stop, and set the parking brake. Stay inside the vehicle. Avoid bridges, overpasses, and power lines.

## After an Earthquake
- Expect aftershocks. Drop, cover, and hold on each time.
- Check yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid.
- Check for gas leaks. If you smell gas, open windows, leave the building, and call the gas company. Do not use matches, lighters, or electrical switches.
- Check for structural damage. Leave if the building looks unsafe.
- Check water and sewage lines. Do not flush toilets until you know sewage lines are intact.
- Use your phone only for emergencies to keep lines open.
- If trapped, do not move. Cover your mouth. Bang on a pipe or wall so rescuers can find you. Do not shout — you may inhale dangerous dust.
