# First Aid Basics for Disaster Situations

## Bleeding
- Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth. Press firmly and do not remove the cloth.
- If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top without removing the first.
- Elevate the injured area above the heart if possible.
- For severe limb bleeding that won't stop, apply a tourniquet 2-3 inches above the wound. Note the time it was applied.
- Seek professional medical help as soon as possible.

## CPR (Adult)
1. Check the scene is safe.
2. Check for responsiveness — tap and shout.
3. Call for emergency help or tell someone to call.
4. Place the person on their back on a firm surface.
5. Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest, other hand on top, fingers interlocked.
6. Push hard and fast — at least 2 inches deep, 100-120 compressions per minute.
7. If trained: give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions. Tilt head back, lift chin, pinch nose, breathe into mouth.
8. Continue until help arrives or the person starts breathing.

## Burns
- Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for at least 10 minutes.
- Do not apply ice, butter, or toothpaste.
- Cover with a loose, sterile bandage.
- Do not pop blisters.
- For chemical burns, remove contaminated clothing and flush with water for 20 minutes.
- For electrical burns, do not touch the person until the power source is off.
- Seek medical help for burns larger than 3 inches, on the face, hands, feet, genitals, or over joints.

## Fractures and Sprains
- Do not move the injured area.
- Immobilize with a splint if possible. Use sticks, boards, or rolled newspaper padded with cloth.
- Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling. 20 minutes on, 20 minutes off.
- Elevate the injured area.
- Do not attempt to realign a bone.

## Hypothermia
- Move the person to a warm, dry area.
- Remove wet clothing.
- Warm the center of the body first: chest, neck, head, and groin.
- Use blankets, body heat, or warm (not hot) beverages if the person is conscious.
- Do not rub the skin or use direct heat.
- Handle the person gently — rough movement can cause cardiac arrest in severe hypothermia.

## Heat Stroke
- Heat stroke is a medical emergency. Body temperature is above 103°F (39.4°C).
- Call for emergency help immediately.
- Move the person to a cooler place.
- Cool rapidly with cold water, ice packs on neck, armpits, and groin, or wet sheets with a fan.
- Do not give fluids if the person is unconscious.

## Choking (Adult)
1. Ask "Are you choking?" If they cannot speak, cough, or breathe:
2. Stand behind the person. Make a fist with one hand.
3. Place the thumb side of your fist just above the navel.
4. Grasp your fist with the other hand.
5. Give quick upward thrusts until the object is dislodged.
6. If the person becomes unconscious, begin CPR.
