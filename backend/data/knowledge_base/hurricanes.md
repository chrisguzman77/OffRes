# Hurricane Safety Guide

## Before a Hurricane
- Know if you are in a hurricane evacuation zone. Visit your local emergency management website.
- Prepare a hurricane kit: water, food, medications, flashlight, battery-powered radio, batteries, cash, important documents, phone chargers.
- Board up windows with plywood or close storm shutters.
- Bring outdoor furniture, trash cans, and loose items inside.
- Fill your car's gas tank.
- Fill bathtubs and large containers with water for sanitation use.
- Identify a safe room in your home — an interior room on the lowest floor with no windows.

## Hurricane Categories
- Category 1 (74-95 mph): Minor damage to roofs, gutters, trees.
- Category 2 (96-110 mph): Major roof and siding damage, trees uprooted, power outages lasting days to weeks.
- Category 3 (111-129 mph): Devastating damage, electricity and water unavailable for days to weeks.
- Category 4 (130-156 mph): Catastrophic damage, most trees snapped, power outages lasting weeks to months. Areas may be uninhabitable.
- Category 5 (157+ mph): Catastrophic destruction. High percentage of homes destroyed.

## During a Hurricane
- Stay indoors and away from windows, skylights, and glass doors.
- Close all interior doors. Brace external doors.
- If the eye of the hurricane passes over, there will be a temporary calm. Do not go outside — the storm will resume with winds from the opposite direction.
- If you lose power, turn off major appliances to reduce the power surge when electricity is restored.
- Use flashlights, not candles, to avoid fire risk.
- If you are in a mobile home, leave and go to a designated shelter. Mobile homes are extremely vulnerable to hurricanes.

## After a Hurricane
- Stay away from downed power lines.
- Do not wade in standing water — it may be electrically charged from downed lines.
- Avoid damaged buildings until inspected.
- Use stored water or boil water until authorities confirm the water supply is safe.
- Take photos of all damage before cleanup for insurance.
- Watch for displaced wildlife, especially snakes, that may have been washed into your area.
