# Evacuation Guide

## When to Evacuate
- When local authorities issue an evacuation order.
- When you feel unsafe even without an official order.
- When you see rising water approaching your home.
- When you smell gas or see structural damage after an earthquake.
- When wildfire smoke is visible or air quality is dangerous.

## Evacuation Planning
- Identify at least two evacuation routes from your home.
- Know the routes from your workplace and children's schools.
- Designate a meeting point outside the disaster area.
- Choose an out-of-town contact person that all family members can check in with.
- Keep gas tank at least half full at all times during disaster season.
- Pack a go-bag in advance.

## Go-Bag Essentials
- Water and water purification (tablets or filter).
- Non-perishable food and a manual can opener.
- First aid kit.
- Medications and copies of prescriptions.
- Copies of important documents (ID, insurance, medical records) in a waterproof bag.
- Cash in small bills.
- Phone charger and portable battery.
- Flashlight and extra batteries.
- Change of clothes and sturdy shoes.
- Map of the area (do not rely on phone GPS).
- Whistle to signal for help.
- Dust mask, plastic sheeting, duct tape.
- Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities.
- Items for infants, elderly, or pets as needed.

## During Evacuation
- Follow designated evacuation routes. Do not take shortcuts — they may be blocked or dangerous.
- Leave as early as possible to avoid traffic and road closures.
- If told to evacuate, do not delay.
- Take your go-bag, pets, and critical items.
- Turn off water, gas, and electricity if time permits and authorities advise it.
- Lock your home.
- Let your out-of-town contact know you are evacuating and your destination.
- Do not drive through standing water.

## After Returning Home
- Wait for official clearance to return.
- Watch for hazards: downed power lines, gas leaks, structural damage, contaminated water, displaced animals.
- Document all damage with photographs.
- Contact your insurance company.
- Do not turn on electrical switches or gas until you have checked for leaks and damage.
