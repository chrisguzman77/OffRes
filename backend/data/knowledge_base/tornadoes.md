# Tornado Safety Guide

## Tornado Warning Signs
- Dark, greenish-colored sky.
- Large hail without rain.
- A loud roar similar to a freight train.
- A visible rotating funnel cloud.
- Debris cloud at ground level even without a visible funnel.

## Before a Tornado
- Know the difference: a Tornado Watch means conditions are favorable. A Tornado Warning means a tornado has been sighted or detected by radar — take shelter immediately.
- Identify your safe room: a basement, storm cellar, or interior room on the lowest floor with no windows (bathroom, closet, hallway).
- Practice tornado drills with your family.
- Prepare an emergency kit.

## During a Tornado
- Go to your safe room immediately.
- If in a house with no basement, go to a small interior room on the lowest floor (closet, bathroom, hallway). Get under something sturdy.
- Cover yourself with a mattress, blankets, or pillows to protect from debris.
- If in a mobile home, get out and go to a nearby sturdy building. Mobile homes are extremely dangerous during tornadoes.
- If caught outside with no shelter, lie flat in a ditch and cover your head. Be aware of potential flooding.
- Never try to outrun a tornado in a car. If caught in a car, park, keep your seatbelt on, duck below the windows, and cover your head.
- Do not seek shelter under highway overpasses — wind speeds increase under them.

## After a Tornado
- Watch for downed power lines and broken gas lines.
- Stay out of damaged buildings.
- Help injured neighbors if you can do so safely.
- Use flashlights, not candles.
- Watch for broken glass and nails.
